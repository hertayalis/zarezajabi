How did Chris Duarte start his Professional Career?

When Chris Duarte was thirteen years old, he started playing basketball. He attended his junior and senior years of high school there with his family when they relocated to New York. Similarly to that, he participated in the regional Jordan Brand Classic game in 2017.

He was the fifth-best player in New York when he graduated from high school. He was given scholarships from several colleges and universities, but he choose to play for Western Kentucky.

Chris joined the basketball team at Northwest Florida State College after enrolling there. He played 23.3 minutes per game, averaging 12.1 points, 6.7 rebounds, and two steals.

Chris Duarte was selected to the first team All-Panhandle Conference after the campaign and assisted his squad in making it to the Elite Eight stage of the NJCAA Division I Championship.

He continued by saying that he would spend his junior year at Oregon University there before starting his sophomore year. As a sophomore, he contributed 19 points, 7.1 rebounds, and 2.5 assists per game. In addition, coaches and members of the media both awarded him Panhandle Player of the Year.

He also moved on to Oregon, where he played against Fresno State in his first collegiate game. The Pac-12 Conference selected him as Player of the Week. The basketball player had an average of 12.9 points, 5.6 rebounds, and 1.7 steals per game toward the end of the season.

He played his first game for Oregon against Fresno State on November 5, 2019. He scored 31 points on December 29 while shooting 12-of-15 from the field and 6-of-9 from three-point range, setting a junior season high. In 2020–2021, Duarte guided the Ducks to the Sweet Sixteen of the NCAA Men’s Division I Basketball Tournament.

He was voted the Jerry West Award winner for the top shooting guard in men’s collegiate basketball on April 3, 2021. He was also selected to the AP Third Team All-American team and the AP Pac-12 Player of the Year. Dana Altman, the head coach of Oregon, said during a news conference on March 29, 2021, that Duarte would declare for the 2021 NBA draft.

The Indiana Pacers choose Duarte with the 13th overall pick in the 2021 NBA Draft. Similarly to that, he joined the Pacers on August 4.

On October 20, he made his NBA debut. In a 123-122 loss to the Charlotte Hornets, he scored 27 points on 6-9 three-point shooting, five rebounds, and a steal, breaking the Pacers team record for rookie debut points. In a three-point loss to the Toronto Raptors on October 30, 2021, Duarte recorded a career-high six assists in addition to 12 points, six rebounds, and a steal.
