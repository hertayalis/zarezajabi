Naomi Watts Wore a Version of Taylor Swifts Vintage-Inspired Shoes

July 2024 · 2 minute read

If there’s one celebrity that does street style right, it’s Naomi Watts. She knows how to fuse together chic and casual pieces, curating a wardrobe that’s timeless — and I want everything in it. Lucky for me (and you!), her recent look is totally attainable.

Spotted on the set of The Friends, Watts wore a double-breasted, olive-colored coat that looked like it was stolen right out of Kate Middleton’s closet. For accessories, she donned a slouchy beret, paisley scarf, ruched bucket bag, and her go-to winter gloves. But I’m particularly fond of her vintage-inspired oxford shoes that give typical flats a run for their money — and I’m adding a pair to my cart as we speak.  

Bruno Marc Classic Oxfords Dress Shoe

Softwalk Whitby Oxford Shoe

Offering a unisex silhouette that boasts everyday elegance, Watts’ shoes are ideal for a multitude of styles. Pair them with a skirt and oversized sweater for a preppy, Taylor Swift-coded look, or consider a band tee and ripped mom jeans for some punk action. Meanwhile, a cashmere sweater and trousers will give your oxfords a classic, sophisticated allure that can’t be beat.     

IMO, oxfords are some of the most creative footwear choices out there. Style them with knee-high or ruffled, ankle-grazing socks, or completely change the look with sheer tights. Simply folding up your pants is another easy idea that puts your shoes on full display. There isn’t a wrong way to make oxford shoes your own, and that’s what I love about them.    

Tons of brands offer spot-on lookalikes, too. Bruno Marc has mastered the silhouette, including a padded insole, grippy bottom, stitched construction, and slight heel — all at the affordable price of $30. Softwalk offers a more elevated option combining a round toe, lace-up design, smooth leather, and two-toned design.

If you can’t wait to secure your own pair of Watts-inspired shoes, then check out the below six picks. Starting at $30, there’s something for everyone.
