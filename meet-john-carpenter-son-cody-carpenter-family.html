Who is John Carpenter son? The facts surrounding the American film director’s kids have generated a positive online buzz, piquing the curiosity of his fans.

Many know John Howard Carpenter as a famous American film director, screenwriter, and composer. Likewise, he is most commonly associated with horror, action, and science fiction films.

Carpenter is mainly recognized as one of the greatest masters of the horror genre.

Also, the French Directors’ Guild gave him the Golden Coach Award. Likewise, he lauded John as “a creative genius of raw, amazing, and spectacular emotions.”

Moreover, the director’s early films included box office hits, including Halloween, Starman, The Fog, and Escape from New York.

Moreover, Carpenter’s other 1970s and 1980s productions became cult classics, including Dark Star, The Thing, Christine, and Prince of Darkness.

Acknowledged as an influential filmmaker, Carpenter returned to the Halloween franchise as an executive producer of the 2018 sequel Halloween (2018).

Also, he released the sequels Halloween Kills (2021) and Halloween Ends in 2022.

Moreover, John Carpenter and his wife, Sandy King, have co-owned a comic book company, Storm King Comics, in 2012. Likewise, this company serves as a division of Storm King Productions.

Meet John Carpenter Son Cody Carpenter

Legendary filmmaker John Carpenter son, John Cody Carpenter, was born on 7 May 1984, in Los Angeles, California. Likewise, Cody is a famous musician and composer.

Moreover, Cody received fame for his work with his father and musician, Daniel Davies. Also, he has worked on several solo projects.

As the son of a famous filmmaker and composer, Cody followed in his father’s footsteps to succeed in Hollywood. 

Cody started taking piano lessons at a young age. Likewise, he was always surrounded by instruments.

Sadly, his parents were separated shortly after his birth. However, he continued his passion as he played at his mother’s and father’s houses.

Carpenter began writing music at 13. Moreover, the famous music composer admits he “didn’t write anything of quality until he was in his mid-20s.”

Cody has been referred to by his middle name since birth to dodge confusion with his father’s similar first name.

Moreover, Cody’s first major musical contributions came in 1998 as he composed for Vampires. Also, his other credits include playing keyboards in Ghosts of Mars in 2001.

In 2005, Cody composed and performed the soundtrack to Pro-Life and Cigarette Burns. Similarly, he composed two episodes in Showtime’s Masters of Horror anthology television series.

Unlike Cody’s composing work and his collaborations with his father, the composer’s solo work falls more into progressive rock and jazz fusion genres.

Furthermore, Cody lists artists like Genesis, ELP, Vince DiCola, and T-Square as major influences.

John Carpenter son released several solo albums exclusively on Bandcamp between 2014 and 2018.

Cody followed up with 2019’s Force of Nature and 2020’s Control. Also, he featured many of the same guest musicians, including John Konesky, Junior Braguinha, and PJ D’Atri.

John Carpenter Family Tree

Filmmaker and screenwriter John Carpenter was born to Milton Jean and Howard Ralph Carpenter on 16 January 1948. Likewise, his family raised him in Carthage, New York.

John’s father, Howard Ralph Carpenter, served as a college music professor. Also, his family moved to Bowling Green, Kentucky, in 1953.

The filmmaker’s father took a job as a music professor at Western Kentucky University after they relocated to Kentucky.

Carpenter’s family raised him in a log cabin on Western Kentucky University’s campus. 

John Carpenter established a legacy which his son, Cody Carpenter, continues. The father-son duo often collaborates on their musical journey in several hit Hollywood films.
