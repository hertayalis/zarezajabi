Steve Scalise biography 

Scalise was born in New Orleans, Louisiana, and is one of three children of Alfred Joseph Scalise, a real estate broker who passed away on October 8, 2015, at the age of 77, and Carol Schilleci. His siblings are Glenn and Tara Scalise. Scalise’s great-grandparents immigrated to the United States from Italy in the late 1800s.

He completed his education at Archbishop Rummel High School in Metairie, Jefferson Parish, and obtained a Bachelor of Science degree in computer science with a minor in political science from Louisiana State University (LSU) in Baton Rouge. During his time at LSU, Scalise was a member of the Acacia Fraternity.

He currently serves on the board of the American Italian Renaissance Foundation, which supports the American Italian Cultural Center. As a member of the Republican Party, Scalise held the position of House Majority Whip from 2014 to 2019 and subsequently served as the House Minority Whip from 2019 to 2023.

Following the removal of Kevin McCarthy as Speaker of the House, a decision that Scalise opposed, he was nominated by House Republicans in the October 2023 Speaker of the United States House of Representatives election.

Prior to his tenure in Congress, Scalise served four months in the Louisiana State Senate and three terms in the Louisiana House of Representatives. He also held the position of chair of the House Republican Study Committee from 2013 to 2014. On June 19, 2014, Scalise was elected majority whip of the United States House of Representatives by his Republican colleagues, assuming office on August 1.

Notably, he is the first Louisiana to hold the position of majority whip since Hale Boggs of Louisiana’s 2nd congressional district from 1962 to 1971. In 2017, following Senator David Vitter’s retirement, Scalise became the dean of the Louisiana congressional delegation. His district encompasses a significant portion of New Orleans’s suburbs, including Metairie, Kenner, and Slidell, as well as a portion of New Orleans itself.
